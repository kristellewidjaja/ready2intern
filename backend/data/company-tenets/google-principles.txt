Google's Evaluation Principles and Cultural Values

Google evaluates candidates, including interns, based on a combination of technical excellence, "Googleyness," and alignment with company values. These principles guide how Google assesses fit and potential.

Core Principles:

1. Focus on the User
Everything we do starts with the user. We build products that people love and that make their lives better. User experience is paramount.

For interns: Demonstrate user-centric design, usability testing, understanding of user needs, and focus on creating delightful experiences.

2. Think 10x, Not 10%
We aim for breakthrough innovations, not incremental improvements. We encourage moonshot thinking and ambitious goals that can change the world.

For interns: Show ambitious projects, innovative thinking, willingness to tackle big problems, and creative solutions that go beyond obvious approaches.

3. Bet on Technical Insights
We believe that technical innovation is the foundation of great products. Deep technical understanding leads to breakthrough solutions.

For interns: Demonstrate strong technical fundamentals, deep understanding of computer science concepts, algorithmic thinking, and ability to apply technical knowledge creatively.

4. Ship and Iterate
We launch products and learn from real-world usage. We believe in getting things out there, gathering feedback, and continuously improving.

For interns: Show completed projects, iterative development, incorporating feedback, and comfort with launching and learning.

5. Give Users What They Want Before They Know They Want It
We anticipate user needs and build products that solve problems users didn't know they had.

For interns: Demonstrate innovative thinking, understanding of user behavior, predictive problem-solving, and ability to identify unmet needs.

6. Be Data-Driven
We make decisions based on data and evidence, not opinions or intuition. We measure everything and let the data guide us.

For interns: Show use of analytics, A/B testing, metrics-driven development, and ability to analyze and interpret data to inform decisions.

7. Collaborate and Innovate
Great ideas come from collaboration. We work together across teams and disciplines to build the best solutions.

For interns: Demonstrate teamwork, cross-functional collaboration, open communication, and ability to work effectively with diverse teams.

8. Aim for Simplicity
We strive to make complex things simple and accessible. The best solutions are often the simplest ones.

For interns: Show ability to simplify complex problems, clean code, intuitive interfaces, and focus on essential features over feature bloat.

---

"Googleyness" - Cultural Fit:

Googleyness is Google's term for cultural fit. It encompasses several key attributes:

1. Comfort with Ambiguity
Ability to thrive in uncertain situations and adapt to changing requirements. Comfortable with open-ended problems.

2. Intellectual Humility
Willingness to admit when you're wrong, learn from others, and recognize that you don't have all the answers.

3. Collaborative Spirit
Genuine enjoyment of working with others, sharing credit, and helping teammates succeed.

4. Conscientiousness
Taking responsibility for your work, following through on commitments, and maintaining high standards.

5. Courage and Resilience
Willingness to take risks, speak up with ideas, and bounce back from failures.

6. Fun and Passion
Enthusiasm for your work, sense of humor, and ability to make work enjoyable for yourself and others.

Technical Excellence at Google:

Google places extremely high value on technical skills:

- Algorithms and Data Structures: Deep understanding and ability to apply them
- System Design: Ability to design scalable, efficient systems
- Code Quality: Clean, efficient, well-documented code
- Problem Solving: Creative and analytical thinking
- Computer Science Fundamentals: Strong foundation in CS concepts
- Learning Ability: Quick to master new technologies and concepts

Evaluation Notes for Internship Candidates:

When evaluating resumes and experiences against Google's principles, look for:

- Focus on the User: User research, usability testing, user-centric design, accessibility considerations
- Think 10x: Ambitious projects, innovative solutions, breakthrough thinking, not just incremental improvements
- Bet on Technical Insights: Strong CS fundamentals, algorithmic solutions, technical depth, optimization work
- Ship and Iterate: Completed projects, iterative development, MVP approach, continuous improvement
- Anticipate User Needs: Innovative features, predictive solutions, understanding of user psychology
- Be Data-Driven: Use of analytics, metrics, A/B testing, data analysis in projects
- Collaborate and Innovate: Team projects, cross-functional work, open source contributions
- Aim for Simplicity: Clean code, simple interfaces, elegant solutions to complex problems

Googleyness Indicators:
- Comfort with Ambiguity: Open-ended projects, self-directed learning, adaptability
- Intellectual Humility: Learning from failures, crediting others, acknowledging limitations
- Collaborative Spirit: Team projects, helping others, sharing knowledge
- Conscientiousness: Completed projects, attention to detail, reliability
- Courage and Resilience: Ambitious projects, recovering from failures, taking risks
- Fun and Passion: Interesting side projects, enthusiasm in descriptions, creative pursuits

For Software Engineering Intern roles (STEP program), focus especially on:
- Strong technical fundamentals (algorithms, data structures, system design)
- Googleyness (cultural fit indicators)
- User focus and impact
- Collaborative and learning mindset
- Code quality and problem-solving ability

Key Differentiators:
- Google values technical depth and CS fundamentals more than most companies
- Googleyness (cultural fit) is weighted heavily in hiring decisions
- 10x thinking and ambitious goals are encouraged
- Data-driven decision making is essential
- Simplicity and user focus are paramount
- Intellectual humility and collaboration are highly valued
