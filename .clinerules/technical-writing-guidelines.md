# Technical Writing Guidelines

## Structure and Organization

### Lead with Key Information
- Start with your main conclusion or recommendation
- Provide supporting details in logical order
- Use clear headings to organize content
- Create scannable content with bullet points and lists

### Logical Flow
- Organize information from general to specific
- Group related concepts together
- Use transitions to connect ideas
- Provide clear next steps or conclusions

## Writing Style

### Clarity and Precision
- Use specific, measurable terms instead of vague language
- Replace "significant improvement" with "25% performance increase"
- Avoid jargon unless necessary for the audience
- Define technical terms when first introduced

### Active Voice and Direct Language
- Use active voice: "The system processes requests" not "Requests are processed"
- Be direct and concise
- Use strong, specific verbs
- Eliminate unnecessary qualifiers

### Professional Tone
- Maintain objective, professional language
- Avoid emotional or subjective language
- Use consistent terminology throughout
- Write for your specific audienceâ€™s knowledge level

## Content Guidelines

### Evidence-Based Writing
- Support claims with specific data and examples
- Cite sources and provide context
- Distinguish between facts and opinions
- Use concrete examples to illustrate concepts

### Reader-Focused Design
- Write for your specific audience
- Anticipate and answer reader questions
- Use formatting to improve readability
- Provide actionable information

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, verify:
- [ ] Main points clearly stated upfront
- [ ] All claims supported by evidence
- [ ] Active voice used throughout
- [ ] Technical terms defined
- [ ] Content organized logically
- [ ] Clear next steps provided
- [ ] Formatting enhances readability
- [ ] Content appropriate for target audience
